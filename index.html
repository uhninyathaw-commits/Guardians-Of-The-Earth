Welcome to The Guardians Of The Earth

Green spaces — such as parks, gardens, wetlands, and eco rooftops — are vital for healthy cities. They clean the air, lower urban heat, protect biodiversity, and give people safe places to relax and connect. Studies show that communities with more greenery enjoy better physical health, reduced stress, and stronger social bonds. This website highlights the environmental, social, and health benefits of green spaces, while encouraging everyone to support and expand them for a sustainable future.



Purpose Of Our Website

The purpose of our website is to educate people about the importance of green spaces in our cities, and to give suggestions on practical and realistic solutions people can use to help increase more green spaces in our cities both as individuals and as a community. Many city organizers and developers mainly focus on urbanizating and they tend to priortize short-term financial gains over long-term investments for our environment and community. As the result, air pollution is getting worse, and the pace of global warming is increasing, as well and many other environmental impacts. Our website will educate readers about this issue, the benefits and solutions for lack of green spaces in cities.

What are green spaces?

Green spaces are areas in cities or towns covered with vegetation—like parks, gardens, street trees, green roofs, and wetlands—that provide environmental, social, and health benefits. They improve air quality, reduce heat, support biodiversity, and enhance mental and physical well-being.Trees and plants reduce the urban heat island effect, cooling cities by up to 10°C compared to paved areas. Green areas absorb rainfall, reducing flood risks and lowering water treatment costs. They serve as the ecological lungs of urban environments by cleaning the air, cooling the streets, absorbing rainwater, and providing habitats for wildlife. They are proven to improve physical health by filtering pollutants and encouraging outdoor activity, while also supporting mental well‑being by reducing stress, anxiety, and depression. Socially, they act as gathering places that strengthen community bonds, and economically they raise property values, attract tourism, and reduce healthcare costs. In short, green spaces balance urban development with nature, making cities healthier, more resilient, and more livable.

Most frequently asked questions :

💭 Why are green spaces important?

They serve as the ecological lungs of urban environments by cleaning the air, cooling the streets, absorbing rainwater, and providing habitats for wildlife. They are proven to improve physical health by filtering pollutants and encouraging outdoor activity, while also supporting mental well‑being by reducing stress, anxiety, and depression.

💭 How do green spaces help the environment?

Green spaces help the environment by absorbing carbon oxide, releasing oxygen, reducing the risks of flooding by absorbing rainwater and cooling down the environment naturally.

💭 Can green spaces help our mental health?

Yes, it's proven that green spaces can reduce anxiety, stress, sadness and help make people feel calmer, happier and in a better mood.

💭 Do green spaces help with climate change?

Yes, green spces can help with climate change by absorbing green house gases, releasing oxygen and cooling down the surrounding. This helps reduce urban heat island effects.

💭 Are green spaces only parks?

No, green spaces include rooftop gardens, mini parks, roadside trees, eco-friendly bus stops, etc. Any spaces with greenery can be considered as green spaces.
