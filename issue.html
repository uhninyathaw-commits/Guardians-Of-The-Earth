Issues With Green Spaces

Understanding the environmental problems affecting green spaces in sustainable smart cities.





Issues caused by lack of green spaces

Pollution

Pollution is one of the biggest threats to green spaces. Air pollution damages trees and plants while water pollution contaminates rivers, lakes, and soil. Pollution also harms animals and reduces biodiversity.

Urbanization

Rapid urbanization causes many parks and forests to disappear because cities continue expanding. Buildings, roads, and factories replace natural areas, making cities hotter and less healthy.

Climate Change

Climate change increases temperatures and causes extreme weather such as floods and droughts. These environmental changes damage green spaces and affect wildlife habitats.

Habitat Fragmentation

Habitat fragmentation happens when large green areas are divided into smaller parts because of roads and buildings. This makes it difficult for animals and insects to survive and move safely.

Noise Pollution

Noise pollution from traffic and construction disturbs birds and wildlife. It also reduces the peaceful environment that green spaces should provide for people.

Lack Of Public Awareness

Many people do not understand the importance of protecting green spaces. Littering, cutting trees, and damaging plants can happen when awareness is low.
